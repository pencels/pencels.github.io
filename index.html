Hello there!

My name is Christopher Pence. I go to school at UC San Diego with a major in Computer Science. Among my interests in CS are linguistics, drawing and graphic design.

This academic year (2017-18), I work for the CSE Department at UCSD, tutoring for CSE 11 (Introduction to CS & OOP with Java). The class is accelerated in pace, but has a lot of fun GUI-based assignments, so I'm looking forward to it.
I was at the department last (2016-17) year to tutor CSE 30 (Computer Organization and Systems Programming). I started tutoring in Fall 2016, working in ARM and SPARC assembly for both versions of the class.

The last few summers (2016, 2017), I worked at the WhizKidz Computer Center as an instructor in web development, game development, and introductory courses to programming languages like Python.

Cool Things

These are projects I am especially proud of, whether or not they may be finished.

EventLIT

Android app made for the CSE 110 software engineering course at UCSD. Provides a platform for UCSD student organizations to post events for students to find and RSVP to. I worked mainly on the back end, managing user authentication and database storage/access.

jas

The assembler for the Janus Instruction Set, a custom ISA developed with my friend @errs.io.

Proof Builder

A "workbench" that facilitates writing proofs for proof-based classes. Won first place in the Education category at Hacktech '16.

Roboto or Notto

Chatbot implemented at Hacktech '17 which takes user input and tries to learn to speak in a more "human" way. Written using a Wolfram back end, and React.
